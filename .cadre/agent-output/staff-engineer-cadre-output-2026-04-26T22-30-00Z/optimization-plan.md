## 2. Prose summary

The hook surface still has the most leverage [1]: the 783 KB events-log on the live filesystem is dominated by Bash and Edit responses that are either recoverable from git (Edit/Write) or not narrative-load-bearing (Bash success). The truncation strategy is structural [2] — JSON-blob truncation strips `file_path` from long Read responses and produces unparseable tail records. There is also a quiet session-boundary leak [3]: UserPromptSubmit fires before the synthesizer's atomic rename, so the next session's first prompt lands in the log being consumed; pairing this with a SessionStart sentinel event [4] makes the session-boundary cut clean for the synthesizer.

On LLM capability surface, the synthesizer's prompt buries its load-bearing rules in the middle third [5] (per *Lost in the Middle*) and underspecifies the highest-stakes output — narrative synthesis [6] (per Chen et al., specification-level technique). The synthesis is also a one-shot where a Reflexion-style self-critique pass against the watchlist [7] would catch anti-pattern hits before the artifact lands.

On harness architecture, the prime hook's 21-line `additionalContext` injection is orchestrator-side bloat [8] for what is mechanically a one-line Agent-tool dispatch — the orchestrator already knows what its workers do per CLAUDE.md "subagents are your senses" doctrine. The synthesizer SOP carries 9 ordered steps when only 3-4 require LLM judgment [9] (per IFScale's instruction-density curve). The PostToolUse matcher is `*` [10], violating context economy at the matcher layer rather than the script layer. The whole prime-hook indirection [11] exists as a ladder around the broken `"type": "agent"` hook (CC issue #39184) and should be tracked for removal once upstream resolves. The synthesizer's tool grants can drop Edit [12]; the multi-entry same-date archive flow has a byte-compare branch [13] that O_APPEND or seq-suffix files would eliminate.

On polish, the prime mechanism is invisible plumbing with no user-visible feedback signifier [14] (Nielsen's 1s response-time limit) and the agent name is hardcoded in two places without a single source of truth [15] — a quiet rename footgun. L1 has the largest concrete-bytes win; L2 [5][6][7] has the largest *quality* win on the synthesized artifact itself, which is what the user actually reads.

## 3. Per-layer confidence

- **L1 (performance / hygiene):** **high** — concrete byte counts measured live (783 KB log, 8.6 KB p95 PostToolUse, 263 KB Read/Glob/Grep share). Recommendations directly attack measured cost surfaces.
- **L2 (LLM capability):** **medium** — grounded in *creation-techniques.md* (Liu et al. position; Chen et al. spec-level; Shinn/Madaan reflexion). Effect sizes from cited papers don't have a Cadre-specific ablation; expect *direction* to hold but *magnitude* to vary. The narrative-quality measurement [6] is the highest-uncertainty piece — variance grading across 3 runs is doable but hasn't been baselined.
- **L3 (harness architecture):** **high** — the prime hook bloat [8], step-count [9], matcher-level filtering [10], and tool-grant tightening [12] are all directly grounded in the agent + hook files and CLAUDE.md doctrine. ADR-073 already named the dispatch shape, so [11] is a tracking item rather than a new proposal.
- **L4 (interaction):** **low-to-medium** — both findings are real but small-blast-radius. The session-start microcopy [14] is genuinely useful; the rename-footgun [15] is a hypothetical that hasn't fired and may not be worth pre-empting until it does (per CLAUDE.md "Don't pre-invent taxonomy").
